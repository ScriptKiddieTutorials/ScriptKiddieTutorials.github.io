1144T Waker Volunteer Camp




Day 1 (7/31)

The first day was pretty chill. When we arrived at the church, to my surprise, I found that both the natural environment and the dormitories were decent, not like the typical volunteer setup at all. If I had known, I wouldn't have brought an extra sleeping bag! The first day was primarily about socializing among the volunteers, such as getting to know new friends and ice-breaking activities. I met Ray, who was studying in Canada and had come back to Taiwan specifically to volunteer for the summer. Quickly, Ray, start chatting with Cola, Van, and me, who already knew each other.

After dinner, we did a few volunteer interaction activities. One activity that left a deep impression on me was the first one: the team leader asked us to make a name tag so that the children could recognize and call us by name. After we finished, the leader wanted everyone to stick their name tags on a poster. Because the poster was deliberately not big enough, we all had to trim our name tags a bit. During this process, I saw volunteers actively collaborating with those around them, which made me feel that everyone in this team was very devoted to finding solutions to problems.

After knowing how to address each other, we played an ice-breaking game. It was quite funny that I couldn't remember everyone's names then. The last activity was the "Little Angel and Little Master" game. I was quite troubled by this because my "Little Master" was a girl I didn't know. I didn't know how to help her. Overall, the first day was very relaxing. I looked forward to meeting the cuties (children) next day!


Day 2 (8/1)

Today started with a volunteer's ice-breaking game. This game was well-designed, allowing the children to get to know each other and us volunteers. It made me realize that a good activity doesn't need to be too complicated; the key is interacting with the children. My first impression of the children was that they were quite cute and more well-behaved and innocent than I had imagined.


Day 7 (8/6)

As the results exhibition concluded, the moment of parting was approaching. After saying goodbye to the church and the pastor, we dragged our large bags to Hualien Station. Upon departure, everyone was reluctant to part and cherished the last moments, taking many photos together. Then, the news came that the train was about to depart, indicating that we had to part with the children. I hugged Jiahe, the children I bonded with the most, and waved goodbye. After taking one last look at the children, I stepped into the train station, and tears welled up in my eyes. On the train, I saw the children waving goodbye to us, jumping up and down enthusiastically. Soon, the train departed, and they chased us until the forest obscured their figures.


Thoughts

This 7-day volunteer camp in Hualien has been one of the most unforgettable experiences of my life. I stepped out of my comfort zone, learned a lot from people, and made lasting connections. As we parted ways, I couldn't help but feel a deep gratitude and nostalgia.
